Description

Sudat is where art and philosophy intertwine, an attempt to understand how the accumulated structures and pressures within society eventually give rise to creative and transformative forces.

About this collection

The Sudat collection endeavors to depict the tension born from "cultural abscesses," compressed within the fabric of society and the psyche of individuals. These works reflect the hidden, internal forces buried beneath layers of tradition, norms, and social constructs, which, under certain conditions, erupt into visible crises.

As Jacques Lacan suggests, these "complexes" are rooted in the collective unconscious and embedded within the symbolic structures of societyâ€”structures that gradually enclose the individual within a web of obligations and limitations. Lacan believed that human psychology is shaped in interaction with these societal constructs, and any deviation from this framework transforms into a form of social conflict.

In Sudat* these forces and pressures are represented as "cultural abscesses" formed beneath the surface of society, manifesting explosively and revealing the hidden dynamics of cultural and social crises.
