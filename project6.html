About this collection

As the artist behind this collection, my aim has been to engage the audience with the gravity of an everyday crisis through a simple statement: *"On average, each person produces 1 kilogram of waste daily."* This phrase, repeated throughout the first piece, mirrors the relentless cycle of consumption and accumulation in our lives. Each repetition acts as a mental fragment of waste, gradually increasing its psychological weight on the viewer.

In the second piece, I sought to visually condense the volume of 1 kilogram of plastic and metal waste into a compact, miniature representation. This reduction in scale symbolizes how these invisible, seemingly insignificant fragments infiltrate our daily lives, masking their true magnitude. By shrinking the actual volume tenfold, I attempt to highlight the stark contradiction between the enormity of their impact and their inconspicuous appearanceâ€”a poignant reminder that what remains unseen is not inconsequential.

Together, these two pieces form a combination of awareness and caution. I want the audience to feel the collective weight of every discarded item: every small piece of trash, every plastic bottle, every disposable package. In the end, they all contribute to a massive mountain of shared responsibility. This collection is an invitation to reflect and reconsider our daily waste production and the responsibility we bear toward the environment.
