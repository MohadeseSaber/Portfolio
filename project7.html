About this collection

The sheqaq collection is an attempt to depict moments of fracture and separation, where cohesion disintegrates, and the fissures become expressions of inner and outer conflict. These works, inspired by the profound meaning of the Persian word "شقاق" (sheqaq)—which translates to rift, split, or separation—metaphorically explore the moments and conditions in which unity and harmony unravel, giving rise to new boundaries.

In these pieces, complex and curved surfaces intertwined with fragmented structures symbolize the struggle to maintain cohesion in the face of opposing forces. Just as *"Schism"* can also signify intellectual or emotional division, this collection delves into the internal challenges of the human psyche, moments of emotional rupture, and the conflicts and differences that create gaps in our connections and perceptions.

*"Schism"* uses intricate forms, curves, and ruptures to encourage contemplation of critical moments and the experience of tension. These works represent the discord and incompatibilities that can evoke both pain and opportunity, suggesting that every rift reflects a crisis while simultaneously providing a space for the emergence of new meaning.
